Petra


Petra, known as the "Rose City" for its pink sandstone cliffs, is an archaeological site in Jordan famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system. Once the capital of the Nabataean Kingdom, Petra was a vital trading hub.

Key Information

  • Location: Jordan
  • Founded: 5th century BCE
  • Famous for: Rock-cut architecture, Al-Khazneh (The Treasury)
  • Culture: Nabataean
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since: 1985
