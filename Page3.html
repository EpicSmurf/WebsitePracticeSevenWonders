Lighthouse of Alexandria


The Lighthouse of Alexandria, also known as the Pharos of Alexandria, was one of the tallest man-made structures of the ancient world. Built on the island of Pharos in Egypt, it served as a landmark and guide for sailors navigating the treacherous waters of the Mediterranean Sea.

Key Information

  • Location: Alexandria, Egypt
  • Height: Estimated between 100-130 meters
  • Built: During the reign of Ptolemy II (3rd century BCE)
  • Purpose: Guide sailors, serve as a landmark
  • Destroyed by: Earthquakes (between 956 and 1323 AD)
