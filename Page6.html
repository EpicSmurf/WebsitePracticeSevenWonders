The Colosseum


The Colosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, is an iconic Roman structure used for gladiatorial contests and public spectacles. It reflects the grandeur and engineering prowess of ancient Rome.

Key Information

  • Location: Rome, Italy
  • Built: 70–80 AD
  • Capacity: Around 50,000–80,000 spectators
  • Purpose: Gladiator games, public events
  • Architectural style: Roman amphitheatre
